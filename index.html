Synthesis
Emily’s User Journey Map: Applying for Benefits While Unemployed 
Main Takeaway From Research and User Journeys
  • 1. Applying for benefits is deeply connected to job loss
  • 2. Time is very important to our target demographic and benefits applications are very time consuming
  • 3. It is very hard to even understand if you qualify for benefits at all, which discourages people from applying
  • 4. Our target demographic lacks the knowledge and resources to break into their desired career fields
These all lead to the Ideation of Three How Might We's
1. How might we make applying for benefits more centralized and time-efficient for low-income populations?
2. How might we help low-income individuals and families more easily understand what benefits they qualify for?
3. How might we help low-income young adults seeking higher-paying jobs break into their desired career fields?

After the feedback we got from testing, we decided that it was best to focus on the chatbot since it seemed as a more realistic and practical way of accomplishing our goal. We iterated on the questions asked according to our feedback...
We decided to build this mid-fi prototype through using Twilio, a cloud communications platform. This allowed us to easily and quickly program sending and receiving text messages with some (but low) costs through their two APIs
Final Changes

No longer using Twilio free trial account = no more free trial message headers and no need for previous registration of phone numbers.

Added in a disclaimer message at the beginning so users know what happens with their data.

Reflections

Takeaway #1:
If you target everyone, you target nobody.
Takeaway #2:
Individuals may be Apprehensive Using Sensitive Private Information for Public Good
Takeaway #3:
Let the Problem Guide Your Process

Advice for Future Designers
  • 1. Design = Language. Be sensitive and respectful of the language you use in your prototypes and presentation.
    Within every question you ask a user, there are biases. Which biases are permissible for your prototype?
  • 2. Iteration will be happening as you submit your final prototype. Remain open and flexible throughout the entire process.
  • 3. Just because a current model exists does not mean that model is best for the demographic. There is no shame in improving, simplifying an existing model graphically or in terms of accessibility and efficiency.
